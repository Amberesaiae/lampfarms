# Broiler Protocol

**Species:** Broiler (Meat Chicken)
**Production Cycle:** 6-8 weeks
**Traditional Remedies:** NO (Commercial focus - pharmaceutical protocols only)

---

## Overview

Broilers are raised for meat production with a short, intensive cycle. Strict pharmaceutical protocols ensure food safety and optimal growth. Traditional remedies are NOT recommended due to:
- Short production cycle (no time for slow-acting remedies)
- Commercial food safety requirements
- Withdrawal period compliance for meat
- Consistent, predictable outcomes needed

## Vaccination Schedule Summary

| Week | Day | Vaccine | Method | Water Amount |
|------|-----|---------|--------|--------------|
| 1 | 7 | Gumboro Intermediate | Drinking water | Half daily |
| 2 | 14 | HB1 (Newcastle + IB) | Drinking water | Half daily |
| 3 | 21 | Gumboro Intermediate Plus | Drinking water | Half daily |
| 4 | 28 | Lasota (Newcastle) | Drinking water | Half daily |
| 5 | 35 | Gumboro Intermediate Plus | Drinking water | Half daily |

**Vaccination Protocol:**
1. Buy vaccine early morning with ice blocks
2. Remove water 2-3 hours before (make birds thirsty)
3. Mix vaccine in half of daily water
4. Ensure all birds drink within 1 hour
5. Give anti-stress next 2 days

## Emergency Protocols (Broilers)

### Coccidiosis Outbreak
```
SIGNS: Bloody droppings, huddling, reduced feed intake

TREATMENT:
Day 1-5: Amprolium - 2 tbsp per gallon (treatment dose)
Day 6-7: Multivitamins - 1.5 tbsp per gallon

WITHDRAWAL: 0-24 hours after last amprolium dose
```

### Respiratory Infection
```
SIGNS: Sneezing, gasping, nasal discharge

TREATMENT (requires vet guidance):
Day 1-5: Tylosin - 1 tsp per gallon
Day 6-7: Multivitamins

WITHDRAWAL: 5 days after last tylosin dose
```

### Heat Stress
```
SIGNS: Panting, wings spread, reduced activity

IMMEDIATE ACTION:
- Electrolytes - 3/4 tsp per gallon
- Increase water availability (2x normal)
- Continue until temperature drops

WITHDRAWAL: 0 days (electrolytes safe)
```
